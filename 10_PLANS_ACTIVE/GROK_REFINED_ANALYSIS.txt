2. Obs/Reward gate desynchronization is real and still present
Your line citations are exact (convoy_env.py:464 vs :375, reward_calculator.py:127). The two latches are deliberately separate:

Observation latch (_braking_received_latched): driven by any hard-brake message (any peer ≤ –2.5).
Reward latch (_hazard_source_braking_latched): gated to the injected source only.

Even in deterministic base_real they are correlated >99 %, but not identical on every transition (mesh relay ordering, one-step lag on the flip). This adds irreducible noise exactly on the critical onset step. The policy sees ego[5]=1.0, but the reward terms may (or may not) fire on that exact transition.
3. Markov property restoration requires more than fixed hazard_step
You are right — fixing hazard_step=200 alone does not make the process fully Markov for the critic. Pre-hazard states at step 50 vs step 150 still look identical in the current 6-dim ego vector, yet remaining horizon (and thus expected penalty exposure) differs. Without explicit time/progress in the observation, the value function still has to infer “how much episode is left” from other noisy signals. Run 011 escaped this only because the massive spike made the remaining horizon irrelevant — the penalty hit instantly.
4. Cone-filter question (the missing piece for sim-to-real)
The documents confirm cone filter is fully implemented in the Python observation pipeline (Phase A, complete Feb 26–28). braking_received is currently computed in convoy_env from the raw received_map before observation_builder. Real ESP32 firmware (ConeFilter + MeshRelayPolicy) and the validate_against_real_data.py path both apply cone filtering to peer messages.
If the bit is not cone-filtered in training, we have re-introduced a subtle sim-to-real gap for distant relayed brakes. We must decide this deliberately.
